## What is a learning architecture?

A learning architecture is the **overall structure of how learning is organized, sequenced, and delivered** across the Sales Academy. It answers:

- What learning formats will we use?
- How are they sequenced over time?
- How do they connect to each other?
- How do we balance formal, social, and on-the-job learning?

---

## The 70/20/10 model as a design principle

The most effective learning architectures don't rely on classroom or e-learning alone. They distribute learning across three modes:

| Mode | % | What it looks like |
|---|---|---|
| **On-the-job learning** | 70% | Real calls, deals, customer interactions, stretch assignments |
| **Social learning** | 20% | Coaching, peer learning, communities of practice, shadowing |
| **Formal learning** | 10% | E-learning, classroom, virtual sessions, certifications |

## Core components of a Sales Academy architecture

### 1. Onboarding path
*For new hires â€” from Day 1 to full performance*

A structured journey that combines product knowledge, sales methodology, tools training, and cultural immersion. Typically spans 30 to 90 days.

### 2. Core curriculum
*For all sales roles â€” the non-negotiable foundation*

A set of essential modules every salesperson must complete, regardless of role or seniority. Typically includes:
- Sales methodology and process
- Product fundamentals
- Customer experience standards
- CRM and tools basics

### 3. Role-specific paths
*Differentiated by role and level*

Modules and programs targeting the specific competency gaps of each profile. A Junior B2C rep has different learning needs than a Senior B2B Account Manager.

### 4. Certification program
*Validating competency at key milestones*

Formal assessments that validate a learner has reached the expected proficiency level. Can be:
- Knowledge-based (quiz, written assessment)
- Skills-based (role play, observed call, manager validation)
- Combined

### 5. Continuous learning layer
*Always-on, pull-based content*

Short, targeted content learners can access anytime: product updates, sales tips, competitive intel, best practice videos. Often delivered via LMS/LXP or mobile app.

### 6. Community & social learning
*Peer-to-peer knowledge sharing*

Best practice communities, internal champions, mentoring programs, team debriefs. Often underinvested â€” and among the highest-impact levers.

## Multi-year Academy roadmap

A Sales Academy is built over time. Here's a typical 3-year vision:

| Year | Focus | Key deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| **Year 1** | Foundation | Competency framework, onboarding path, core e-learning modules, LMS launch |
| **Year 2** | Depth | Role-specific paths, certification program, coaching integration, community launch |
| **Year 3** | Excellence | Advanced programs, manager academy, measurement maturity, content refresh cycle |
